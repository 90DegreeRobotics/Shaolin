# Shaolin Kung Fu Study Guide
## Integrated Reference for Solo Practice and Chirox

This guide integrates the supplied `Shaolin Kung Fu Study Guide.md` material
into the repo's current truth system.

It is not a lineage claim. It is not a substitute for a teacher. It is a
structured reference for the solo practitioner using this repository, the Dojo
Record, and Chirox.

The hard boundary stays intact: Chirox may measure, record, retrieve, read, and
compare. Chirox may not invent form authority.

## Safety First

The Pain Rule and Beginner Rule in `SAFETY.md` outrank everything here.

- Static holds may be measured by Chirox when the body is in frame.
- Flowing sequences may be recorded and measured for range of motion.
- Kicks, sweeps, staff forms, and acrobatics are not graded by Chirox.
- Sharp pain, numbness, dizziness, joint instability, or panic ends the drill.
- A wooden staff in public is still a public-safety and public-perception issue.

## Martial Virtue for the Autodidact

Without a teacher standing in the room, the practitioner has to internalize the
teacher's first job: restraint.

For this repo, martial virtue means:

- Train for steadiness, not intimidation.
- Use public material respectfully and do not borrow lineage authority.
- Keep a written record instead of trusting heroic memory.
- Prefer fewer movements done honestly over technique collecting.
- Stop when the safety rule says stop.

## Digital Resource Discipline

Open video instruction is useful only when it becomes a governed syllabus.

Use external instructors by role, not by endless browsing:

| Need | Use the resource for | Do not use it for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stance depth and meaning | Wu Bu Quan, Ma Bu, Gong Bu, Pu Bu, Xu Bu | Certifying your form without feedback |
| Kicks and Ji Ben Gong | Slow biomechanics, names, sequence order | Jumping into advanced kicks cold |
| Qi Gong | Breath-led warm-up, recovery, joint-friendly practice | Medical claims or energy absolutism |
| Staff | Grip, flowers, basic strikes, spatial awareness | Public performance or weapon fantasy |

The rule is simple: watch once, practice slowly, record what happened, then
return to the same lesson. The feed is not the teacher. Repetition is.

## The 18 External Basics

These are reference movements, not a day-one workout. They should be staged
through the one-year path and scaled aggressively.

| # | Movement | Pinyin | Training role | Chirox role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Horse Stance Punch | Ma Bu Dan Bian | root, leg endurance, waist-to-fist connection | measurable hold: `horse` or `horse_guard` |
| 2 | Bow Stance Punch | Gong Bu Xie Xin | linear drive, hip rotation, rear-leg rooting | measurable hold: `bow` |
| 3 | Low Stance Chop Down | Pu Bu Qie Zhang | low mobility, hip/adductor opening, descending strike | shallow hold: `drop_stance`; strike is human-reviewed |
| 4 | Cat Stance Block | Xu Bu Liang Zhang | empty front foot, evasion, balance | measurable hold: `empty_stance` |
| 5 | Twisting Low Punch | Xie Bu Chong Quan | crossed-leg recovery, low-level transition | record only; no Chirox grade |
| 6 | Front Stretch Kick | Zheng Ti Tui | hamstring and hip-flexor dynamic stretch | range-of-motion recording only |
| 7 | Low Snap Kick | Di Tan Tui | knee extension speed, low-line control | record only |
| 8 | High Slap Kick | Dan Pai Jiao | hand-eye-foot timing, explosive lift | range-of-motion recording only |
| 9 | Side Thrust Kick | Ce Chuai Tui | chamber, hip rotation, heel line | record only; do not grade |
| 10 | Back Kick | Hou Deng Tui | rear-line power, turning awareness | record only |
| 11 | Outside Crescent Kick | Wai Bai Jiao | lateral arc, hip mobility | range-of-motion recording only |
| 12 | Inside Crescent Kick | Li He Jiao | adductor line, inward arc | range-of-motion recording only |
| 13 | Inside Kick Block | Gua Mian Jiao | defensive arc toward head line | record only |
| 14 | Block-Punch-Kick Combination | Zheng Pi Tui | linking upper defense and lower offense | record only |
| 15 | Front Sweep | Qian Sao Tui | ground-level rotation and Pu Bu control | human-reviewed only; high injury risk |
| 16 | Scorpion Kick / Tail Sweep | Xiezi Wei | evasive hook and rear-line mobility | human-reviewed only; advanced |
| 17 | Squat Jump Double Kick | Er Ti Jiao | plyometric power and timing | advanced; do not self-score |
| 18 | Tornado Kick | Xuanfeng Jiao | spinning aerial coordination | advanced; do not self-score |

Progression rule: static base first, then slow transitions, then low kicks, then
larger kicks, then sweeps, then aerial work. If the stance collapses, the
advanced movement has not been earned.

## Wu Bu Quan

Wu Bu Quan links five core stances into one short form:

- Gong Bu, bow stance
- Ma Bu, horse stance
- Pu Bu, drop stance
- Xu Bu, empty/cat stance
- Xie Bu, crossed/resting stance

Chirox can help with the static checkpoints around the form. The form itself is
recorded, watched, and compared over time. It is not graded as correct by the
machine.

Suggested use:

1. Hold each stance separately for short rounds.
2. Record one slow Wu Bu Quan pass.
3. Watch the video once without judgment.
4. Write one correction in the Dojo Record.
5. Repeat the same correction for a week.

## Luohan and Qi Gong Balance

The incoming study guide paired the 18 external basics with Luohan/Qi Gong work.
That pairing is correct for this repo's safety logic: hard work without recovery
becomes injury, and soft work without discipline becomes fog.

Keep the benefits language grounded:

| Pattern family | Use it for | Repo boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lifting / pressing the sky | warm-up, shoulder line, breath timing | Chirox may measure arms-raised posture |
| Bow-drawing patterns | horse stance endurance, chest/back opening | measurable as horse/arms position only |
| Turning / circling patterns | neck, spine, waist mobility | record range of motion; no medical claims |
| Punching patterns | intent, breath, relaxed structure | do not use as impact training early |
| Crane / balance patterns | leg control, patience, nervous-system steadiness | Chirox may measure `crane` or `one_leg_stand` |
| Mountain / carrying patterns | shoulder and back endurance | measured only when static and visible |

The Eight Brocades remain the repo's first Qi Gong sequence. Luohan references
may be studied after the daily invariant is stable.

## Staff Work

The staff is the first weapon only after the empty-hand base is honest.

For year one, staff work means:

- staff as shoulder opener
- grip changes
- figure-eight flowers at slow speed
- basic chop, sweep, and thrust lines
- stance-with-staff integration
- outdoor clearance and public safety

Do not begin with speed. Do not begin near people. Do not begin indoors under a
low ceiling. A broken lamp is not training feedback; it is just bad planning.

Chirox role:

- record staff practice as `staff_basics`, `staff_flowers`, or `staff_form`
- measure general joint range where visible
- keep the video timeline
- never declare a staff form correct

## Outdoor Training in Normal and Bloomington

Outdoor work is useful for space, footing, and the psychological shift of
leaving the room. It also adds public responsibility.

Current official-rule snapshot checked July 16, 2026:

| Area | Current official rule to respect |
| --- | --- |
| Town of Normal public parks | Public parks list hours as 6 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Constitution Trail is dawn to dusk; vehicles stay on designated roadways/parking areas; firearms are prohibited; nuisance-gathering fines can apply to qualifying group conduct. |
| Maxwell Park | Official facility page lists 307 N. Parkside Rd, 6 a.m. to 9 p.m. daily, 125 acres, sports facilities, natural area, and hiking trails. |
| Bloomington parks | Park regulations list 6 a.m. to 10 p.m., no alcoholic beverages, no marijuana, firearms prohibited, vehicles limited to roadways/parking areas, and pets leashed. |
| Constitution Trail | Useful for walking/running and linear footwork only; keep right, yield, signal, leash pets, and do not block the trail. |

Training use:

- Maxwell Park: open-space conditioning, walking, stance rounds, non-flashy staff
  basics only when isolated and lawful.
- Constitution Trail: walking, running, breath pace, linear stepping. No wide
  staff work.
- Bloomington parks: check the specific facility before carrying equipment.

Practical rule: if another person could reasonably read the movement as a
threat, scale it down or move. Public training should make the path cleaner, not
make strangers manage your intensity.

```powershell
chirox debrief --question "How should I begin Wu Bu Quan without hurting my knees?"
chirox debrief --question "What should staff practice look like in quarter two?"
```

Record flowing or staff work without pretending to grade it:

```powershell
chirox record --exercise wu_bu_quan --seconds 120
chirox record --exercise staff_basics --seconds 180
```

Train measurable foundations:

```powershell
chirox train --stances horse,bow,empty_stance --seconds 45
chirox train --stances drop_stance,crane,horse_guard --seconds 30
```

## The Integration Rule

Every new technique must land in one of three states:

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Measurable | Chirox has deterministic geometry for it and can speak limited corrections. |
| Recordable | Chirox can film/log/range-measure it, but only a human eye judges form. |
| Deferred | The movement is too advanced, risky, or noisy for current solo practice. |

Do not force a movement across that boundary to make the system look stronger.
The boundary is the protection.
