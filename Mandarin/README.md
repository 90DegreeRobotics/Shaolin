# Mandarin Study Hall
## Open Learning Lane for Language, Reflection, and Calligraphy

This folder is the Mandarin study hall for the Shaolin year.

The purpose is not fluency theater. The purpose is daily contact with a language that carries many of the concepts used in the practice: Dao, Qi, Ren, Gong Fu, Wu Wei, Chan, Ma Bu, Jing, Ming, and the other terms that become thinner when handled only in English.

The method is deliberately mechanical:

1. Listen.
2. Speak.
3. Read pinyin with tones.
4. Read characters.
5. Write by hand.
6. Use spaced repetition.
7. Tie one word or phrase to the daily Dojo Record.

This is not a separate hobby. It is part of the evening forge.

## Where This Fits in the Day

The [Temple Day](../TEMPLE_DAY.md) gives Mandarin three contact points:
the Morning Mouth (10 minutes of tones and shadowing aloud after breakfast),
the Training Tongue (counting reps and naming stances in Mandarin during
Kung Fu — zero extra minutes), and the Evening Hand (Anki, character
writing, and the journal anchor before the evening sit).

## Daily Minimum

Do this even on tired days:

```text
1. Review 10 Anki cards.
2. Listen to one short Mandarin audio clip or sentence.
3. Write one character 9 times.
4. Add one English thought and one Mandarin anchor to the journal.
```

If you cannot do all four, write one character once and say it aloud. Keep the ember alive.

## First Character

Begin with:

```text
道
dào
path, way, road, principle
```

The path is not understood by staring at the gate. Walk.
