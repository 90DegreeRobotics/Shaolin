# The Food Catalog
## The Concrete List: What to Eat on This Path

[README.md](README.md) is the law of this lane — principles, rhythm, the
quarters. This file is the *pantry made explicit*: the actual foods, tiered by
how freely they belong on the plate. Print it, shop from it, stop improvising.

How to read the tiers:

- **Foundation foods** — the plate is built from these. Eat daily, freely,
  within the 80 percent rule.
- **Deliberate foods** — good fuel with a purpose or a portion limit. Chosen
  consciously, not drifted into.
- **Occasional foods** — legitimate, but weekly-ish, not daily.
- **Excluded by default** — not sins; friction the practice does not need.

Nothing here is medical prescription. Adjust with qualified help for any real
medical condition.

## Foundation Foods — Vegetables

Target five or six kinds across the day. The list is long on purpose — variety
comes from rotation, not from sauce.

**Leafy and green:** bok choy, napa cabbage, regular cabbage, spinach, kale,
chard, romaine and leaf lettuce, celery, Chinese broccoli (gai lan), broccoli,
green beans, snap peas, snow peas, asparagus, zucchini, cucumber, okra,
bean sprouts.

**Roots and hard vegetables:** carrots, daikon radish, red radish, beets,
turnips, onions, leeks, scallions, garlic, ginger, bamboo shoots, winter melon,
bitter melon, eggplant, bell peppers, tomatoes, cauliflower.

**Mushrooms:** shiitake (fresh or dried), button, oyster, king oyster, wood
ear, enoki. Meaty texture, deep flavor, no downside — use them hard.

**Sea vegetables:** kelp/kombu, wakame, nori. Traditional, mineral-rich
(iodine), excellent in soups. A sheet of nori is a legitimate snack.

Preparation default: steamed, stir-fried in a little oil, blanched, or in
soup. Freshness and variety carry the flavor; heat and sauce do not.

**Note on the five pungents** (garlic, onions, chives, leeks, scallions):
foundation foods in this pragmatic lane. Strict monastic tradition excludes
them — see "On Tradition" in [README.md](README.md). If you adopt that, adopt
it knowingly as tradition, not as nutrition.

## Foundation Foods — Fruit

Whole fruit, especially around training. Two to four pieces or cups a day.

Apples, pears (Asian pears included), bananas, oranges and all citrus, grapes,
watermelon and melons, peaches, plums, apricots, kiwi, mango, pineapple,
pomegranate, all berries (fresh or frozen — frozen is equally good and
cheaper), jujube (red dates — the traditional congee fruit), goji berries
(small amounts, congee and tea), hawthorn.

**Dried fruit** (raisins, dates, dried apricots) is *deliberate*, not
foundation: dense sugar, easy to overrun. A small handful in congee or before
training — measured, not from the bag.

## Deliberate Foods — Animal Protein

The conscious tier. This is not a loophole; it is a tool. Use it deliberately
when the training load asks for it — which, for a deconditioned starter
holding protein high in a deficit (see "Starting Overweight" in
[README.md](README.md)), is *most days*, in modest portions.

| Food | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| Eggs | the simplest complete protein; boiled eggs are a portable snack |
| Salmon | protein + omega-3 (EPA/DHA); canned is fine and cheap |
| Sardines / mackerel | canned, cheap, omega-3 dense, no cooking |
| White fish (cod, tilapia, sole) | lean, light, steams beautifully |
| Canned tuna | convenient lean protein; moderate frequency |
| Shrimp | very lean, fast-cooking |
| Chicken breast / thigh | skinless default; thigh for flavor |
| Turkey | lean ground turkey stretches any dish |
| Lean pork (loin, tenderloin) | traditional in Chinese home cooking |
| Lean beef | iron-dense; especially useful in a deficit |
| Plain yogurt / Greek yogurt | unsweetened only; add your own fruit |
| Cottage cheese / quark | high protein per calorie |
| Kefir / milk | if dairy sits well with you |

Guidance, not law: one palm-sized portion at lunch (the main meal) and eggs
or dairy at one other meal covers most training days. Fish twice a week
covers omega-3 without supplements.

## Occasional Foods

Weekly-ish, eaten with full attention, ideally inside a shared or celebratory
meal: dumplings and other filled doughs, sushi, dark chocolate (a few squares,
70%+), cheese beyond cottage/yogurt, red meat beyond the lean cuts, baked
goods you or someone you know actually made, restaurant meals generally. The
Quarter 4 skill — deviating deliberately and returning without sliding — is
practiced here.

## Excluded by Default

Sugary drinks and sodas, energy drinks, fruit juice as a habit (whole fruit
instead), candy and dessert as routine, pastries and packaged baked goods,
chips and ultraprocessed snacks, instant noodles as a staple, deep-fried
anything as routine, processed meats (bacon, sausage, deli) as routine,
alcohol as fuel or habit, heavy cream sauces, anything with an ingredient
list you would not read aloud in the Dojo.

Not sins. Friction. Each one either damages recovery, agitates appetite, or
trains the exact inattention the rest of the day is trying to untrain.

## Training-Day Additions

**Before training (30-90 min prior), pick one:**
- banana, or two dates
- small bowl of rice or oats
- toast with honey
- a few dried apricots and a handful of nuts (longer sessions)

**After hard training (within ~2 hours), protein + carbohydrate:**
- eggs over rice with scallions
- soy milk + banana (blend with peanut butter for more)
- Greek yogurt with fruit and oats
- tofu or chicken over rice with vegetables — i.e., just eat lunch/dinner

**During:** water; salt if sweating heavily. Nothing else needed at this
volume.

## The Congee Formula (Eight-Treasures Pattern)

The traditional breakfast, systematized. Pick and simmer (or slow-cook
overnight; 1 part dry mix to 7-8 parts water):

```text
2 grains:    rice + millet, or rice + barley, or oats + rice
1 bean:      adzuki, mung, or black bean (pre-soaked)
1-2 nuts:    walnut, peanut, or chestnut
1 seed:      sesame, pumpkin, or chia
1 fruit:     jujube dates, goji, or raisins (small amount)
optional:    pumpkin/sweet potato cubes, ginger slice
finish:      pinch of salt OR teaspoon of honey — not both meals the same way
protein boost: stir in silken tofu, soy milk, or top with a boiled egg
```

## Meal Templates

**Breakfast (pick one):**
1. Eight-treasures congee + boiled egg
2. Oatmeal with soy milk, banana, walnuts, chia
3. Rice porridge with silken tofu, scallion, sesame oil, nori

**Lunch — the main meal (formula: grain + palm of protein + 3 vegetables):**
1. Rice + seared tofu + stir-fried bok choy, mushrooms, carrots
2. Rice + steamed fish with ginger-scallion + greens + winter melon soup
3. Noodle bowl + chicken + napa cabbage, bean sprouts, snap peas
4. Rice and beans + eggs + roasted broccoli and peppers

**Dinner — light (formula: light grain + vegetables + small protein):**
1. Soba noodles in miso broth with wakame, tofu cubes, scallion
2. Vegetable soup with white beans + slice of whole-grain bread
3. Small rice + stir-fried greens + two eggs
4. Congee (thin) + pickled vegetables + edamame

## Protein Cheat Sheet

While deconditioned and losing fat, protein is the non-negotiable (README,
"Starting Overweight"): roughly 1.6-2.2 g per kg of *goal* body weight daily,
spread across the three meals. Rough per-serving counts:

| Serving | Protein (approx.) |
| --- | --- |
| 3 eggs | 18 g |
| Palm of chicken/fish/lean meat (120 g) | 25-30 g |
| Block of firm tofu (300 g) | 24 g |
| 100 g tempeh | 19 g |
| 1 cup cooked lentils | 18 g |
| 1 cup cooked black beans | 15 g |
| 1 cup Greek yogurt | 17-20 g |
| 1 cup cottage cheese | 25 g |
| 1 cup edamame | 17 g |
| 1 cup soy milk | 7-8 g |
| 2 tbsp peanut butter | 8 g |
| Can of sardines | 20-23 g |

Three meals hitting 30-40 g each clears the bar. If a day runs plant-only,
stack soy hard (tofu + soy milk + edamame) and mind the gap list below.

## The Plant-Leaning Gap List

If animal foods become rare in your pattern, cover these deliberately
(README, "Fueling the Forge"):

- **B12:** fortified soy milk or nutritional yeast — or a simple supplement.
  Non-negotiable on a fully plant pattern; food willpower does not make B12.
- **Iron:** lentils, tofu, pumpkin seeds, beans + a vitamin-C food in the
  same meal (peppers, citrus) to absorb it.
- **Omega-3:** ground flax, chia, walnuts daily; fish twice weekly closes it
  fully, algae oil if fully plant-based.
- **Total energy:** plants are big and filling per calorie. Under a hard
  training load, undereating happens *by accident*. Nuts, rice, and soy milk
  are the honest volume levers.

## Weekly Shopping Skeleton

The boring, reliable basket. Adjust quantities to appetite and load:

```text
Grains:     rice (big bag), oats, noodles, bread
Legumes:    2 dry or canned beans, red lentils, tofu x3, soy milk x2
Vegetables: 2 leafy (bok choy, spinach), 1 cabbage, carrots, onions,
            ginger, garlic, scallions, mushrooms, 2 rotating others
Fruit:      bananas, apples/oranges, 1 rotating, frozen berries, jujubes
Protein:    eggs (dozen+), canned sardines/salmon, 1-2 fresh choices
            (fish/chicken/lean pork), yogurt
Fats:       peanut butter, walnuts or almonds, sesame (oil or paste)
Drinks:     green tea, one other tea
```

One shop, one list, no wandering the snack aisles. The pantry is a form too —
practice it the way you practice the stances: the same, daily, until it is
invisible.
