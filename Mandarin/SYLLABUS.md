# Mandarin Syllabus
## One-Year Open Learning Lane

## Purpose

This syllabus teaches enough Mandarin to support the Shaolin year: pronunciation, tones, pinyin, high-value characters, basic sentence patterns, listening discipline, and calligraphy-linked reflection.

The goal is not to perform fluency. The goal is to build daily contact with the language and to understand the core terms of the practice with more respect.

## Standards

By the end of the first year, the practitioner should be able to:

- pronounce pinyin and tones carefully enough to keep learning without corrupting the foundation
- recognize and handwrite a small set of personally meaningful characters
- maintain a simple Anki deck
- read basic Mandarin sentences with pinyin support
- understand the working meaning of core Shaolin/Daoist/Confucian terms
- write a nightly English-to-Mandarin journal anchor
- use open dictionaries and stroke-order tools without pretending machine translation is authority

## Daily Time

Minimum: 10 minutes.

Recommended: 20 to 30 minutes.

Long session: 60 minutes once per week.

## Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| Monday | Pronunciation and tones |
| Tuesday | Pinyin and listening |
| Wednesday | Characters and stroke order |
| Thursday | Core vocabulary and Anki |
| Friday | Sentence patterns |
| Saturday | Longer study or copying practice |
| Sunday | Review, journal audit, deck cleanup |

## Year Structure

### Phase 1: Days 1-30 - Sound and Ritual

- Learn initials, finals, tone marks, tone numbers, and basic tone contour.
- Build the nightly calligraphy journal.
- Learn 10 to 20 core characters.
- Create the first Anki deck.
- Use pinyin without shame. Accuracy first.

### Phase 2: Months 2-3 - Basic Sentences

- Learn pronouns, basic verbs, question particles, numbers, dates, and simple negation.
- Read short sentence pairs.
- Start listening repetition with slow audio.
- Expand to 50 to 100 characters or words.
- Continue the nightly English -> Mandarin journal anchor.

### Phase 3: Months 4-6 - Practical Language

- Work through beginner lessons from MIT OCW or FSI-derived materials.
- Practice greetings, self-introduction, time, location, food, directions, and daily actions.
- Keep reviews small enough to sustain.
- Begin copying short sentences by hand.

### Phase 4: Months 7-9 - Reading and Listening Contact

- Use sentence collections for daily reading.
- Add audio-backed cards when available.
- Practice shadowing: listen, pause, repeat.
- Write one original simple Mandarin sentence per week, then verify it.

### Phase 5: Months 10-12 - Integration

- Maintain daily Anki.
- Maintain calligraphy journal.
- Read simple Mandarin with pinyin support.
- Build a personal glossary of Shaolin-year terms.
- Write the Day 365 reflection with English body and Mandarin anchors.

## Assessment

Monthly checks:

```text
Can I hear the tones better than last month?
Can I pronounce this week's characters without guessing?
Can I write my core characters from memory?
Did Anki become a servant or a burden?
Did Mandarin deepen the journal, or become decoration?
```

If the answer becomes vanity, reduce scope. The language serves the path.
