# Safety
## Practice Boundaries for the Shaolin Year

This repository is not medical advice, therapy, physical therapy, martial lineage instruction, or a substitute for a qualified teacher.

This project is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or representative of Shaolin Temple, Songshan Shaolin Temple, any Shaolin lineage, any monastery, or any living teacher. The word Shaolin is used here as a public discipline frame: Chan, Wu, Yi; mind, body, conduct, recovery.

## Before You Train

If you have serious joint injuries, chronic pain, heart disease, dizziness, fainting episodes, uncontrolled blood pressure, severe anxiety, panic attacks, trauma responses, severe depression, or any medical condition that makes exertion risky, consult qualified help before increasing intensity.

The practice should make you more stable over time, not more damaged.

## The Pain Rule

Use this rule every session.

### Green

Continue with attention.

- muscular effort
- heat
- shaking
- breath challenge
- boredom
- restlessness
- mild fatigue that resolves with rest

### Yellow

Reduce, scale, or switch practice.

- joint discomfort
- pinching
- numbness
- dizziness
- unusual anxiety
- form collapse
- pain that changes movement quality

Actions:

- reduce stance depth
- shorten duration
- slow down
- use chair or wall support
- switch to walking, breathing, or gentle Qi Gong
- write down what happened

### Red

Stop.

- sharp pain
- chest pain
- faintness
- radiating pain
- loss of balance
- panic escalation
- pain that changes your gait
- symptoms that persist or worsen after practice

Do not negotiate with red. Stop, record it, recover, and seek qualified help if the symptom is severe or persistent.

## Beginner Rule

Begin lower than pride wants.

A ten-second honest Horse Stance is better than a two-minute collapse. A five-minute walk done daily beats one heroic session followed by four dead days.

The first victory is showing up tomorrow.

## Recovery Rule

Recovery is part of training.

- Aim for 7.5 to 8 hours of sleep when possible.
- Take at least one lighter day per week.
- Deload every 6 to 8 weeks.
- Do not stack hard sessions on poor sleep for multiple days.
- Persistent dread, irritability, joint pain, insomnia, or falling performance means reduce intensity.

## AI and Physical Truth

No generative AI form diagnosis.

An AI may help log practice, compare recorded measurements, ask questions, and summarize patterns. It may not claim your stance or posture is correct without evidence.

Physical assessment must be grounded in:

- video evidence
- deterministic measurement
- explicit uncertainty
- qualified human correction when available

The body is not a prompt. The body is ground truth.

## Public Reader Rule

If you are using this repository alone, go slowly.

Do not copy intensity. Copy honesty.
