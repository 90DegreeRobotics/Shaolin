# Mandarin Curriculum
## First 90 Days, Then the Year

This curriculum is built for a beginner with paper, pencil, and limited daily time.

Reference files this curriculum leans on: [PINYIN.md](PINYIN.md) for the sound
system, [CHARACTERS.md](CHARACTERS.md) for stroke order and radicals,
[SPEAKING.md](SPEAKING.md) for the output ladder, and
[VOCABULARY.md](VOCABULARY.md) for the staged word lists. Weeks 1-2 below run
alongside the 10-day pinyin bootcamp in PINYIN.md.

## Daily Session Template

```text
2 minutes - pronunciation or listening
3 minutes - Anki review
3 minutes - character writing
2 minutes - journal anchor
```

On longer days, expand each block. On difficult days, keep the order and shrink the duration.

## Weeks 1-4: Sound, Pinyin, and the Journal Gate

### Week 1 - The Path Character

- Learn pinyin tone marks, tone numbers, and the five tone categories: first, second, third, fourth, neutral.
- Start the nightly journal template.
- Character focus: 道.
- Build Anki cards for: 道, 气, 心, 身, 静.
- Practice saying each word aloud with its tone before writing it.

### Week 2 - Stillness and Breath

- Drill initials and finals for 5 minutes per day.
- Character focus: 静.
- Add: 明, 和, 行, 仁, 忍.
- Listen to one slow Mandarin clip or sentence daily.
- Journal question: where did I become quieter today?

### Week 3 - People and Conduct

- Learn basic pronouns: 我, 你, 他, 她, 我们.
- Learn simple verbs: 是, 有, 去, 来, 学.
- Character focus: 仁.
- Write one English conduct sentence nightly and anchor it in one character.

### Week 4 - First Review

- Review all first 10 characters.
- Clean the Anki deck: suspend cards that are wrong, bloated, or confusing.
- Copy one short sentence three times.
- Monthly check: did the practice remain small enough to survive?

## Weeks 5-8: Basic Sentence Bones

### Week 5 - Identity

- Sentence pattern: 我是...
- Learn name and identity phrases.
- Character focus: 我.
- Journal anchor: one sentence about who you acted like today, not who you imagined yourself to be.

### Week 6 - Negation and Choice

- Learn 不 and 没.
- Practice simple contrasts: I do / I do not, I have / I do not have.
- Character focus: 不.

### Week 7 - Time and Return

- Learn today, tomorrow, yesterday, morning, evening.
- Connect language study to the Dojo Record.
- Character focus: 日.

### Week 8 - Location and Direction

- Learn here, there, inside, outside, up, down.
- Character focus: 中.
- Copy one simple sentence from an open course or sentence collection.

## Weeks 9-12: Listening and Practical Contact

### Week 9 - Food and Water

- Learn basic food, water, tea, eat, drink.
- Character focus: 水.
- Practice one mindful eating sentence.

### Week 10 - Body and Training

- Learn body, hand, foot, breath, pain, tired.
- Character focus: 身.
- Add body-state cards tied to the pain rule.

### Week 11 - Questions

- Learn 吗, 什么, 哪儿, 谁.
- Character focus: 问.
- Write one question to yourself each night.

### Week 12 - First 90-Day Audit

- Review all cards.
- Copy the 10 most meaningful characters.
- Record yourself speaking the starter vocabulary.
- Write a 90-day reflection in English with 3 Mandarin anchors.

## Months 4-6: Course Spine

The course spine (MIT OCW Chinese I or FSI Standard Chinese, from
[RESOURCES.md](RESOURCES.md)) now sequences the grammar and dialogues; this
curriculum sets the pace and the checks. Vocabulary target:
[VOCABULARY.md](VOCABULARY.md) Stage 3.

### Month 4 - Greetings and Identity
- Course spine: first lessons — greetings, names, nationality, courtesy.
- Vocabulary: greetings/courtesy block; keep 5-10 new cards per day.
- Speaking: begin scripted self-talk (SPEAKING.md L3) — three fixed phrases.
- Writing: one copied sentence per week becomes two.
- Check: can you introduce yourself aloud, from memory, with honest tones?

### Month 5 - Time and the Day
- Course spine: numbers in context, dates, clock time, daily schedule.
- Vocabulary: the time block — today, tomorrow, morning, evening, hours.
- Speaking: narrate the Temple Day bells in Mandarin ("现在五点半。我起床。").
- Writing: begin writing the date line of the nightly journal in characters.
- Check: can you say your daily schedule in five simple sentences?

### Month 6 - Food and the Table
- Course spine: food, ordering, likes and dislikes.
- Vocabulary: the food/drink block — this is the Diet lane in Mandarin.
- Speaking: name everything on your plate once a day; 慢慢吃, 八分饱.
- Writing: label the week's shopping list with the characters you know.
- Check (90-day-style audit): record a 60-second self-introduction covering
  name, day, training, and food. File it next to the month-3 recording.

## Months 7-9: Sentence Immersion

Reading and listening volume rise; grammar glue gets installed. Vocabulary
target: Stage 4.

### Month 7 - Sentence Mining
- Tatoeba (or the course's sentence banks): 5 sentences read aloud daily.
- Add audio-backed cards where possible; retire dead cards.
- Speaking: begin weekly recorded monologues (SPEAKING.md L5), 60 seconds.
- Grammar: 了, 的, and measure words 个/杯/碗/次 — from the course spine.

### Month 8 - Wanting, Being Able, Comparing
- Grammar: 想/要/会/能/可以, 比 comparisons, 太...了.
- Speaking: live narration (L4) during chores and walks.
- Writing: one original verified sentence per week becomes three.
- Listening: one slow dialogue daily, shadowed through the full protocol.

### Month 9 - Connected Speech
- Grammar: 因为...所以..., 但是, 也/都/还.
- Speaking: monologues grow to 90 seconds; listen back to month-7 files.
- Reading: first graded-reader attempt or long Tatoeba chains.
- Check: write and speak a five-sentence account of one training session,
  verified against a dictionary, without translation software doing the work.

## Months 10-12: Personal Glossary

The language turns inward toward the practice itself. Vocabulary target:
Stage 5 — one practice-glossary term per week, lived before it is carded.

### Month 10 - The Pillars in Their Own Words
- Weekly glossary term into the journal: where I lived it, where I failed it.
- Begin reading pillar terms in context (Wisdom texts have bilingual editions;
  keep pinyin support without shame).
- Maintain: Anki daily, weekly monologue, nightly journal anchor.

### Month 11 - Consolidation
- Cold-write audit: all Stage 0-2 characters from memory, on paper.
  Anything missed re-enters rotation.
- Prune the deck to what the year actually used.
- Speaking: one monologue on the hardest month of the year, kept.

### Month 12 - The Day 365 Reflection
- Build the final glossary: practice terms, body terms, conduct terms,
  recovery terms, emotional audit terms — each with a one-line lived meaning.
- Prepare the Day 365 reflection:
  - English body
  - Mandarin anchors
  - 12 selected characters from the year, hand-written, with what each
    asked of you
- Record the final monologue: 2 minutes, same prompt as month 7. Listen to
  both. That distance is the year, made audible.

## Deck Rule

If Anki becomes a swamp, reduce new cards to zero and review only. A smaller deck you actually use is superior to a grand deck that defeats you.
