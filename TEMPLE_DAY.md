# The Temple Day
## Daily Structure Modeled on the Shaolin Temple Rhythm

The manual gives the year its shape ([1yeartoShaolin.md](1yeartoShaolin.md)). This
document gives the *day* its shape. A day without structure leaks; a day with a
bell does not negotiate.

This schedule is modeled on the commonly documented daily rhythm of Shaolin
warrior-monk training: rise before dawn, train before breakfast, main meal at
midday, rest, a long afternoon session, a light evening meal, and quiet study
and meditation before an early sleep. Details vary by era and school, and this
project is not affiliated with any temple or lineage. We borrow the *skeleton*
because it works: it front-loads discipline, puts food where the work is, and
ends the day in stillness instead of stimulation.

## The Traditional Pattern (Reference)

The full documented shape, annotated. Two vocations live inside one temple
and one clock: the scholar monks (文僧 wénsēng), whose day centers on
liturgy and scripture, and the warrior monks (武僧 wǔsēng), whose day
centers on training. Both share the bells, the meals, the labor, and the
evening stillness. Accounts differ in detail by era and source — this is the
common skeleton, honestly labeled as a reconstruction, not a lineage claim.

```text
~04:30-05:00  The board (打板 dǎbǎn) and bell circuit wake the compound.
              Earlier in summer, later in deep winter. Rise, dress, assemble.
05:00-06:00   First practice, by vocation:
              - wénsēng: morning liturgy (早课 zǎokè) — sutra chanting in
                the main hall, ~1 hour of voice, breath, and attention.
              - wǔsēng: the morning run (跑山 pǎoshān) — mountain paths
                and stone stairs, then basics in the training yard.
06:00-06:30   Basics (基本功 jīběngōng) for the training track:
              leg presses/stretching (压腿 yātuǐ), swinging kicks
              (踢腿 tītuǐ), waist work (腰功 yāogōng), stance holds
              (站桩 zhànzhuāng). Flexibility first, while the day is young.
06:30-07:15   Breakfast (早斋 zǎozhāi): congee, steamed buns (馒头 mántou),
              pickled vegetables. Taken as a formal meal (过堂 guòtáng):
              silence, a verse of gratitude, nothing wasted, the bowl
              finished clean. The meal is a ceremony of attention.
07:15-08:00   Transition: cleaning of quarters and halls, duties assigned.
08:00-11:30   The long morning block:
              - wǔsēng: technique — basics drilled in lines by rank, forms
                (套路 tàolù) repeated dozens of times, weapons (器械 qìxiè),
                partner drills. Seniors correct juniors; teaching is duty.
              - wénsēng: scripture study, recitation, calligraphy, temple
                administration.
              - all, in rotation: communal labor (出坡 chūpō) — fields,
                kitchen, firewood, carrying water. The Chan farming rule of
                Baizhang stands behind it: 一日不作，一日不食 — "a day
                without work is a day without food." Labor IS practice.
11:30-12:30   Lunch (午斋 wǔzhāi): the main meal — rice, vegetables, tofu,
              soup. Formal guòtáng again. Strict vinaya observance stops
              eating after noon (过午不食 guòwǔ bùshí); hard-training monks
              take the evening meal anyway, and name it honestly (below).
12:30-14:00   Midday rest (午休 wǔxiū): a nap, quiet reading, mending,
              laundry. Scheduled, not stolen. The body absorbs the morning.
14:00-17:00   Afternoon training — the hard session:
              - conditioning: body hardening (排打 páidǎ), sandbag work,
                iron-arm and iron-palm drills for seniors
              - endurance: long stance holds, low walking, stair repeats
              - forms at full intensity, corrected and repeated
              - sparring (散打 sànda) by level and permission
              - flexibility again at the end, while the body is warm
17:00-17:30   Wash. Evening liturgy (晚课 wǎnkè) for the scholar track.
17:30-18:30   Dinner: light — noodles, vegetables, broth. Called the
              "medicine meal" (药石 yàoshí): eaten not for pleasure but as
              medicine for a body that trains. The name itself is a teaching.
19:00-21:00   Evening cultivation: seated meditation (坐禅 zuòchán), sutra
              recitation, a master's instruction (开示 kāishì), and private
              review of the day's corrections.
21:00-21:30   Night bell and board circuit. The silence signal
              (止静 zhǐjìng): talk ends, lamps go out, the compound rests.
```

Rhythms above the day: on the new moon and full moon (初一 and 十五 of the
lunar month) the schedule bends toward ceremony; festival days and retreat
periods alter it further; summer rises earlier and winter later. The modern
wushu schools around the mountain run secularized versions of the same
skeleton — proof that the shape survives the removal of any single belief.

The pattern has five laws worth stealing:

1. **The body works before the world wakes.** The first session happens
   before anything can interrupt it — and flexibility comes first, strength
   later, every single day.
2. **Food follows work.** The biggest meal sits beside the biggest effort;
   the evening meal is small, medicinal, and named so nobody lies about it.
3. **Everything is practice.** Chanting trains breath. Meals train
   attention. Chores train humility and the back. There is no dead time in
   the day, only changes of form.
4. **Rest is scheduled, not stolen.** The nap and the night silence sit in
   the timetable with the same authority as the hard session. Recovery is
   an appointment, not a lapse.
5. **The day closes down, not up.** No stimulation after dark. The mind is
   walked down to rest like a horse cooled after a ride, and the closing
   bell is as absolute as the waking one.

## The Practitioner's Temple Day

This is the full template for a day you control (weekend, remote work, day off).
The compressed version for heavy workdays follows. **The times are the form;
hold the form even when the durations shrink.**

### Morning Gate — 05:30 to 08:00

```text
05:30  Rise. Feet on floor within one minute. Cold water on the face.
       Reset the room to readiness (60 seconds — see manual, Living Area).
05:40  Sit. Breath meditation, 10 minutes.
05:55  MORNING SESSION (40-60 min):
       - Joint warm-up, 5 min
       - Ba Duan Jin / Eight Brocades, full set
       - Stance work: Wu Ji, then Ma Bu holds (scale per phase)
       - Light conditioning or a walk/easy run
06:55  Wash. Dress in the training uniform for the day.
07:10  Breakfast: congee or grain bowl (see Diet/FOODS.md).
       No screen. Eat at 80 percent.
07:35  MANDARIN — MORNING MOUTH (10 min):
       Tones and pinyin drilled ALOUD while the voice is fresh.
       (See Mandarin/PINYIN.md and Mandarin/SPEAKING.md.)
07:50  Set the day's intention: one line, spoken or written.
```

### Work Block — 08:00 to 12:00

The monks did chores and scripture here. You do your profession. Work is
practice: single-tasked, phone out of reach, one thing at a time.

- Mid-morning: stand up once per hour. 10 slow Qi Gong reps or one 30-second
  stance hold at least twice during the block.

### Midday — 12:00 to 14:00

```text
12:00  LUNCH — the main meal of the day. Largest plate, most protein.
       Eaten slowly, utensils down between bites.
12:40  Rest: 20-minute nap OR a slow silent walk. Not a feed. Not a screen.
13:10  Return to work.
```

### Afternoon — 14:00 to 17:00

Work block two. Same discipline. If energy dips, the answer is water, a walk,
or 10 breaths — not sugar.

### The Forge — 17:00 to 18:30

```text
17:00  MAIN TRAINING SESSION (60-90 min, scaled to phase):
       - Warm-up and mobility, 10 min
       - Stance curriculum: Ma Bu, Gong Bu, Pu Bu progressions
       - Form work / technique of the current phase
       - Conditioning finisher (only if recovery allows)
       - Cool down, slow breathing, 5 min
       COUNT EVERY REP IN MANDARIN. Name each stance aloud in Mandarin
       as you enter it. This is the "training tongue" — the second
       Mandarin contact of the day. (See Mandarin/VOCABULARY.md, Stage 1.)
18:15  Rehydrate. Post-training food if the session was hard.
```

The Pain Rule and the Beginner Rule govern this block absolutely. Deconditioned
means the durations shrink; it does not mean the block moves or disappears.

### Evening Gate — 18:30 to 21:30

```text
18:30  Dinner: light. Noodles or grain, vegetables, modest protein.
19:15  Free hour: family, chores, one intentional media window if chosen.
20:15  MANDARIN — EVENING HAND (15-20 min):
       - Anki review (10 cards minimum)
       - One character written 9 times, correct stroke order
       - New material per the week's curriculum
       (See Mandarin/CURRICULUM.md and Mandarin/CHARACTERS.md.)
20:40  Journal: the nightly page (Mandarin/JOURNALING_METHOD.md).
       Physical truth, emotional truth, conduct truth, one Mandarin anchor.
20:55  Evening sit: 5-10 minutes of breath. Close the day's account.
21:10  Screens off. Room reset for tomorrow. Stretch or read paper.
21:45  Lights out.
```

Lights out at 21:45 and rise at 05:30 gives the manual's 7.5-8 hour window.
The morning bell is won or lost the night before.

## The Three Mandarin Contacts

The language is not a separate hobby bolted onto the day. It touches the day
three times, in three different channels:

| Contact | When | Channel | Duration |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Morning Mouth | after breakfast | speaking: tones, pinyin, shadowing | 10 min |
| Training Tongue | during the forge | speaking: counting reps, naming stances | 0 extra min |
| Evening Hand | before the journal | reading/writing: Anki, characters, journal anchor | 15-20 min |

Total: 25-30 deliberate minutes, plus free repetition inside training. On a
collapsed day, keep one contact. The smallest acceptable version: count one
set of anything in Mandarin and write one character once.

## The Compressed Workday

For days the world owns most of your hours. The blocks keep their order and
their names; only the durations shrink.

```text
05:45  Rise. Face. Room reset.
05:55  Sit 5 min.
06:00  Morning session, 25 min: Brocades (short set) + stance holds.
06:30  Wash, breakfast, Morning Mouth 5 min (tone drill aloud).
       — the world's hours —
17:30  The Forge, 40 min. Count in Mandarin.
18:30  Light dinner.
20:30  Evening Hand 10 min: Anki + one character x9.
20:45  Journal page. Evening sit 5 min.
21:15  Screens off.
21:45  Lights out.
```

## The Collapsed Day

Illness, travel, catastrophe. The temple day reduces to the manual's Daily
Invariant, plus one language ember:

```text
1. Sit: 3 minutes of breath.
2. Stand: 1 honest stance hold or 10 slow Qi Gong reps.
3. Serve: 1 deliberate act of respect or restraint.
4. Record: 3 lines in the Dojo Record.
5. Ember: 1 character, written once, spoken aloud with its tone.
```

Five minutes total. There is no day too broken for five minutes.

## Weekly Shape

| Day | Training emphasis | Mandarin emphasis |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | Stances + conditioning | Pronunciation and tones |
| Tuesday | Qi Gong depth + mobility | Pinyin and listening |
| Wednesday | Stances + form work | Characters and stroke order |
| Thursday | Conditioning | Vocabulary and Anki |
| Friday | Form work + light stances | Sentence patterns |
| Saturday | Long session (the full Temple Day) | Long study: copying, shadowing, recording |
| Sunday | Active recovery: walk, stretch, easy Brocades | Review, deck cleanup, weekly character |

Sunday also carries the Weekly Dojo Review from the manual. Every 6-8 weeks a
deload week softens the forge but never removes the bells.

## Rules of the Bell

1. **The schedule is a form.** You practice it like a stance: imperfectly,
   daily, with correction and without drama.
2. **Move a block, never delete it.** If life collides with the forge, the
   forge shrinks or shifts — it does not vanish.
3. **Protect the gates.** The first hour and the last hour of the day belong
   to the practice. Everything in between can flex.
4. **No new inputs after the Evening Gate.** The day closes down, not up.
5. **Missed bell protocol:** note it in the Dojo Record without self-attack,
   and answer the next bell. The path is measured in returns, not in streaks.
