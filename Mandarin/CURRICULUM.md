# Mandarin Curriculum
## First 90 Days, Then the Year

This curriculum is built for a beginner with paper, pencil, and limited daily time.

## Daily Session Template

```text
2 minutes - pronunciation or listening
3 minutes - Anki review
3 minutes - character writing
2 minutes - journal anchor
```

On longer days, expand each block. On difficult days, keep the order and shrink the duration.

## Weeks 1-4: Sound, Pinyin, and the Journal Gate

### Week 1 - The Path Character

- Learn pinyin tone marks, tone numbers, and the five tone categories: first, second, third, fourth, neutral.
- Start the nightly journal template.
- Character focus: 道.
- Build Anki cards for: 道, 气, 心, 身, 静.
- Practice saying each word aloud with its tone before writing it.

### Week 2 - Stillness and Breath

- Drill initials and finals for 5 minutes per day.
- Character focus: 静.
- Add: 明, 和, 行, 仁, 忍.
- Listen to one slow Mandarin clip or sentence daily.
- Journal question: where did I become quieter today?

### Week 3 - People and Conduct

- Learn basic pronouns: 我, 你, 他, 她, 我们.
- Learn simple verbs: 是, 有, 去, 来, 学.
- Character focus: 仁.
- Write one English conduct sentence nightly and anchor it in one character.

### Week 4 - First Review

- Review all first 10 characters.
- Clean the Anki deck: suspend cards that are wrong, bloated, or confusing.
- Copy one short sentence three times.
- Monthly check: did the practice remain small enough to survive?

## Weeks 5-8: Basic Sentence Bones

### Week 5 - Identity

- Sentence pattern: 我是...
- Learn name and identity phrases.
- Character focus: 我.
- Journal anchor: one sentence about who you acted like today, not who you imagined yourself to be.

### Week 6 - Negation and Choice

- Learn 不 and 没.
- Practice simple contrasts: I do / I do not, I have / I do not have.
- Character focus: 不.

### Week 7 - Time and Return

- Learn today, tomorrow, yesterday, morning, evening.
- Connect language study to the Dojo Record.
- Character focus: 日.

### Week 8 - Location and Direction

- Learn here, there, inside, outside, up, down.
- Character focus: 中.
- Copy one simple sentence from an open course or sentence collection.

## Weeks 9-12: Listening and Practical Contact

### Week 9 - Food and Water

- Learn basic food, water, tea, eat, drink.
- Character focus: 水.
- Practice one mindful eating sentence.

### Week 10 - Body and Training

- Learn body, hand, foot, breath, pain, tired.
- Character focus: 身.
- Add body-state cards tied to the pain rule.

### Week 11 - Questions

- Learn 吗, 什么, 哪儿, 谁.
- Character focus: 问.
- Write one question to yourself each night.

### Week 12 - First 90-Day Audit

- Review all cards.
- Copy the 10 most meaningful characters.
- Record yourself speaking the starter vocabulary.
- Write a 90-day reflection in English with 3 Mandarin anchors.

## Months 4-6: Course Spine

- Work through MIT OCW Chinese I or FSI Standard Chinese material slowly.
- Add no more than 5 to 10 new cards per day.
- Keep listening daily.
- Begin weekly sentence copying.

## Months 7-9: Sentence Immersion

- Use Tatoeba or another open sentence source for daily reading.
- Add audio-backed cards where possible.
- Start shadowing short phrases.
- Write one verified Mandarin sentence per week.

## Months 10-12: Personal Glossary

- Build a Shaolin-year glossary:
  - practice terms
  - body terms
  - conduct terms
  - recovery terms
  - emotional audit terms
- Prepare a Day 365 reflection with:
  - English body
  - Mandarin anchors
  - 12 selected characters from the year

## Deck Rule

If Anki becomes a swamp, reduce new cards to zero and review only. A smaller deck you actually use is superior to a grand deck that defeats you.
