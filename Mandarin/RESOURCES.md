# Mandarin Resources
## Open-Source, Open-Data, and Openly Available Tools

This resource map favors open-source and open-data tools. Some excellent learning materials are openly available rather than strictly open-source; those are labeled honestly.

## Core Tools

### Anki

- Link: <https://apps.ankiweb.net/>
- Role: spaced repetition.
- Status: open-source desktop flashcard program; the project page also links to source/contribution paths.
- Use: create a personal deck for characters, pinyin, audio, and journal-anchor words.

### AnkiDroid

- Link: <https://github.com/ankidroid/Anki-Android>
- Role: Android review client.
- Status: open-source.
- Use: optional phone-based reviews, but do not let phone review become scrolling.

## Open Course Spine

### MIT OpenCourseWare - Chinese I

- Link: <https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/21g-101-chinese-i-regular-fall-2014/>
- Role: beginner course structure.
- Status: openly available courseware.
- Use: pronunciation, cultural competence, basic course progression.

### MIT OCW - Learning Chinese: A Foundation Course in Mandarin

- Link: <https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/res-21g-003-learning-chinese-a-foundation-course-in-mandarin-spring-2011/pages/online-textbook/>
- Role: online textbook and multi-semester foundation material.
- Status: openly available courseware.
- Use: structured reading and grammar support after the first weeks.

### FSI Standard Chinese: A Modular Approach

- Link: <https://fsi-languages.yojik.eu/languages/FSI/fsi-chinese-mandarin.html>
- Role: drill-heavy audio/text course.
- Status: public-domain/government-origin materials mirrored in an accessible form.
- Use: listening, pattern drills, and practical spoken language.

## Dictionary and Data

### CC-CEDICT

- Link: <https://www.mdbg.net/chinese/dictionary?page=cedict>
- Role: Chinese-English dictionary data.
- Status: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 data.
- Use: verify words, pinyin, and meanings before adding cards.

### Tatoeba

- Link: <https://tatoeba.org/en/>
- Role: sentence and translation collection.
- Status: open collaborative sentence data under Creative Commons licenses.
- Use: sentence mining after basic pinyin and pronunciation are underway.

## Characters and Stroke Order

### Hanzi Writer

- Link: <https://hanziwriter.org/>
- Role: stroke-order animation and writing practice.
- Status: open-source JavaScript library; uses Make Me a Hanzi data.
- Use: verify stroke order before copying a character.

### Make Me a Hanzi

- Link: <https://github.com/skishore/makemeahanzi>
- Role: character dictionary and graphical stroke-order data.
- Status: open-source/open-data project.
- Use: future local tooling for character sheets or stroke-order references.

## Practical Rule

Do not hoard resources. Choose:

```text
1 course spine
1 dictionary
1 spaced repetition tool
1 stroke-order tool
1 paper journal
```

That is enough.
