# Vocabulary
## The Staged Word Hoard for the Shaolin Year

This is the year's vocabulary, staged to match the curriculum phases. The
rules from [CURRICULUM.md](CURRICULUM.md) govern everything here: 5-10 new
cards per day maximum, every entry verified in CC-CEDICT before it becomes a
card, and every word must be *speakable* (correct tone, aloud) before it is
*card-able*.

Do not import this file into Anki wholesale. It is a quarry, not a deck. Cut
stones as the weeks call for them.

Pinyin note: entries show dictionary tones. Live tone changes (你好 → ní hǎo,
不是 → bú shì, 一次 → yí cì) follow the sandhi rules in
[PINYIN.md](PINYIN.md).

## Stage 0 — The Anchor Characters (Week 1 onward)

The ten journal anchors from [JOURNALING_METHOD.md](JOURNALING_METHOD.md):
道 静 仁 忍 明 行 心 身 气 和. They are the spine of the whole lane; every
other stage hangs off them.

## Stage 5 — The Practice Glossary (Months 10-12)

The philosophical working vocabulary of the six pillars. These are learned
last on purpose: by now every one of them should name something you have
*done*, not something you have read about. One per week, into the journal,
with a page on where you lived it and where you failed it.

| Hanzi | Pinyin | Working meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 道 | dào | the way, path, underlying order |
| 德 | dé | virtue, integrity in action |
| 仁 | rén | humaneness, benevolence |
| 义 | yì | rightness, duty |
| 礼 | lǐ | ritual propriety, respect made visible |
| 智 | zhì | wisdom |
| 信 | xìn | trustworthiness |
| 忍 | rěn | endurance, forbearance |
| 静 | jìng | stillness |
| 定 | dìng | stability, meditative settledness |
| 慧 | huì | insight |
| 禅 | chán | meditation; the Chan (Zen) tradition |
| 无为 | wúwéi | non-forcing, unstrained action |
| 阴阳 | yīnyáng | complementary opposites |
| 丹田 | dāntián | the lower belly center of breath work |
| 精气神 | jīng qì shén | essence, breath-energy, spirit |
| 武德 | wǔdé | martial virtue — the ethics of skill |
| 苦 | kǔ | bitterness, hardship (吃苦 chīkǔ: "to eat bitterness," to endure hard training) |
| 恒心 | héngxīn | perseverance of heart |
| 平常心 | píngchángxīn | ordinary mind, equanimity |

吃苦 deserves special attention: "eating bitterness" is the exact Chinese
name for what this whole year is. When the training is hardest, you finally
know what the word means. That is Stage 5 working as designed.

## Card Discipline (Restated)

- 5-10 new cards a day, maximum. Zero on overloaded days.
- Both directions for characters you must write; recognition-only is
  acceptable for Stage 4 glue words.
- Every card spoken aloud when reviewed — see [SPEAKING.md](SPEAKING.md).
- If the deck becomes a swamp: new cards to zero, review only, prune weekly
  until it serves you again. A small living deck beats a grand dead one.
