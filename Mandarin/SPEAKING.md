# Speaking
## Training the Mouth Like the Body

Speech is a physical skill. The tongue, lips, and breath are muscles executing
forms. You do not learn Ma Bu by reading about Ma Bu, and you will not learn
spoken Mandarin by reviewing flashcards silently. **Everything in this lane is
done aloud.** A silent Mandarin session is a stance practiced lying down.

The good news: physical skills respond to exactly the training you are already
doing — small daily reps, honest self-observation, filmed checks, and patience.

## The Output Ladder

Climb one rung at a time. Each rung is trained daily until it feels boring,
then the next rung is added on top (never instead — lower rungs remain as
warm-up).

```text
L0  Tone drills          single syllables + tone pairs, hand gestures
L1  Echo words           hear a word, repeat it, match the tone exactly
L2  Shadow sentences     speak along with slow audio, catching its rhythm
L3  Scripted self-talk   fixed daily phrases said from memory
L4  Live narration       describing what you are doing, simply, as you do it
L5  Recorded monologue   60-90 seconds of free speech, weekly, kept on file
L6  A living listener    tutor or exchange partner (when ready — months 7+)
```

Where you should be, roughly: L0-L1 in month 1, L2 by month 2-3, L3 by month
4, L4 by month 6, L5 from month 7, L6 when L5 stops being terrifying.

## The Shadowing Protocol (L2)

Shadowing is the core speaking technology of this entire year. Use slow,
clear audio: FSI drills or MIT OCW dialogues from
[RESOURCES.md](RESOURCES.md) are built for this.

```text
1. LISTEN once, doing nothing. Just hear the shape.
2. LISTEN again reading the pinyin. Mark the tones you didn't catch.
3. ECHO: play one phrase, pause, repeat it aloud. Loop 3 times.
4. SHADOW: play it and speak WITH it, slightly behind, like singing
   along. Loop until your mouth stops tripping.
5. SOLO: say it alone, from the pinyin, at the audio's speed.
6. Once a week: record step 5 and compare against the source.
```

One sentence done through all six steps beats ten sentences listened to
passively. This is the language equivalent of one honest stance hold versus
ten sloppy ones.

## The Recording Rule (L5)

The camera does not negotiate, and neither does the microphone. From month 2:

- **Weekly**, during the Saturday long session, record yourself speaking:
  first months, the week's vocabulary and tone pairs; later, a 60-90 second
  monologue (what you trained, what you ate, what hurt, what you learned).
- Keep every file. Name them by date.
- Once a month, listen to a recording from 4+ weeks ago. This is where
  progress becomes audible — and where tone corruption gets caught before it
  fossilizes.

The Dojo already taught you this: self-sense lies, video does not. The ear
version is identical.

## Training-Hall Mandarin (The Training Tongue)

This is the integration point with Kung Fu, and it costs zero extra minutes.
From week 1, the training session itself becomes Mandarin practice:

### Count every rep in Mandarin

```text
一 yī, 二 èr, 三 sān, 四 sì, 五 wǔ, 六 liù, 七 qī, 八 bā, 九 jiǔ, 十 shí
```

Every Brocade repetition, every second of a stance hold you count out, every
set — counted in Mandarin, aloud or whispered. By month 2 the numbers are not
vocabulary anymore; they are reflexes. (Watch 四 sì vs 十 shí — falling vs
rising, s vs sh. Getting these crisp IS pinyin training.)

### Name the stance as you enter it

```text
马步 mǎbù        horse stance
弓步 gōngbù      bow stance
仆步 púbù        drop stance
虚步 xūbù        empty/cat stance
歇步 xiēbù       resting stance
无极 wújí        wu ji standing posture
```

Say it, then sink into it. The word and the form weld together.

### Command yourself

Use these as your internal (or out-loud) coaching voice during sessions:

```text
开始 kāishǐ         begin
停 tíng             stop
休息 xiūxi          rest
再来一次 zài lái yí cì   one more time / again
慢一点 màn yìdiǎn   slower
呼吸 hūxī           breathe
放松 fàngsōng       relax
坚持 jiānchí        hold on / persist
好 hǎo              good
不好 bù hǎo         not good
加油 jiāyóu         keep going (lit. "add oil")
```

By month 3, an entire training session can run on Mandarin self-commands.
That is thirty-plus real, embodied, spoken repetitions per day that no
flashcard can provide.

## Scripted Self-Talk (L3)

Fixed phrases said from memory at fixed moments of the
[Temple Day](../TEMPLE_DAY.md). Start with three; add one per week; verify
every script against a dictionary or course source before adopting it.

```text
On rising:        我起床了。Wǒ qǐchuáng le.         I'm up.
                  新的一天。Xīn de yì tiān.          A new day.
Before training:  我们开始吧。Wǒmen kāishǐ ba.       Let's begin.
During a hold:    呼吸。放松。Hūxī. Fàngsōng.        Breathe. Relax.
After training:   今天练了。Jīntiān liàn le.         Trained today.
                  我很累，但是很好。Wǒ hěn lèi,      I'm tired, but it's good.
                  dànshì hěn hǎo.
At meals:         慢慢吃。Mànmàn chī.                Eat slowly.
                  八分饱。Bā fēn bǎo.                Eighty percent full.
Before sleep:     今天结束了。Jīntiān jiéshù le.     Today is finished.
                  明天再来。Míngtiān zài lái.        Come again tomorrow.
```

## Live Narration (L4)

From month 5-6: while cooking, cleaning, or walking, narrate in baby-simple
Mandarin. 我喝水 (I drink water). 我洗碗 (I wash dishes). 我去走路 (I go
walk). Grammar sloppiness is acceptable here — this rung trains *retrieval
speed and nerve*, not correctness. Correctness lives in L2/L3; nerve lives
in L4. You need both, so keep them separate on purpose.

## The Weekly Speaking Rhythm

| Day | Speaking work (inside the normal Mandarin blocks) |
| --- | --- |
| Monday | L0 tone pairs + one deadly-pair contrast drill |
| Tuesday | L2 shadowing, one new sentence |
| Wednesday | say every written character aloud as it's written |
| Thursday | L1 echo the week's new vocabulary |
| Friday | L2 shadow + L3 run all self-talk scripts once |
| Saturday | recording day: L5, plus review one old recording monthly |
| Sunday | light: run the self-talk scripts, nothing new |

## Honest Failure Modes

- **Silent studying.** The default failure. If a session made no sound, it
  was a reading session, not language training.
- **Speed vanity.** Speaking fast with flattened tones is not fluency; it is
  corruption at velocity. Slow and toned beats fast and toneless, always.
- **Skipping the recordings** because your own voice is uncomfortable. That
  discomfort is the ego demon from the manual wearing headphones. Record
  anyway.
- **Waiting to be "ready" for a listener.** L6 will never feel earned. When
  weekly monologues become routine, find a tutor or exchange partner and be
  bad in front of a human. That is the speaking equivalent of the one-year
  gate: the form tested under contact.
