# Access and Inspiration
## Why This Repository Exists

My interest in Shaolin Kung Fu and Shaolin-informed discipline was awakened in part by the public teaching presence of Master Shi Heng Yi.

I respect his work. I hope one day to attend formal training through his school or another qualified teacher.

This project is not affiliated with Master Shi Heng Yi, Shaolin Temple Europe, Shaolin Temple, Songshan Shaolin Temple, any Shaolin lineage, any monastery, or any living teacher. It is not an endorsement, representation, reconstruction, or replacement of their teachings. It does not reproduce paid course materials.

Official public references:

- Master Shi Heng Yi: <https://www.shihengyi.online/>
- Shaolin Temple Europe: <https://www.shaolintemple.eu/index.php?page=english>

## The Access Problem

I created this because access is unequal.

Some people can afford courses, travel, books, teachers, equipment, stable internet, private space, and time away from survival.

Others are trying to build themselves from the floor, with little money, limited transportation, no stable library access, and no realistic path into formal training.

This repository is for that second person.

## The Purpose

The goal is to provide a free, plain-language, safety-first starting structure for people who need discipline, health, reflection, and daily return, but cannot afford expensive programs or polished systems.

This repository gathers openly available concepts, beginner practices, public resources, personal synthesis, and practical safeguards into a path that a Western English-speaking beginner can understand without pretending to possess lineage or mastery.

Living teachers matter. Formal training matters. Books, temples, schools, and direct correction matter. This project does not replace any of them.

It is only a gate for people who otherwise might have no gate at all.

## The Boundary

This project must never use another teacher's name as armor.

Respect does not create affiliation.

Inspiration does not create authority.

Public learning does not grant lineage.

Open access does not excuse copying protected material.

The path here is humble: learn what can be learned openly, practice safely, tell the truth about limits, and seek qualified correction when it becomes possible.
